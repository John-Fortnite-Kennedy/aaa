In most study domains, developing theories is a significant objective, and the importance of theory in a research undertaking cannot be emphasized. The purpose of this paper is to synthesize a multidisciplinary study of theory creation into a framework that will aid in the development and assessment of theory in project management research. Prescriptive, descriptive, predictive, and practical theories are among the four types of theory provided. To show how the framework may be used retroactively, four "classic" articles have been chosen. The research concludes with a more in-depth consideration of the ongoing debate in the project management discipline about how to improve theory.

The purpose of this research is to acquire a better knowledge of information systems (IS) project management by looking at the ISO 21500:2012/PMBoK 5 procedures that project managers utilize in these projects. According to replies to a worldwide web-based survey, processes from knowledge areas such as scope management, cost management, and time management are extensively used. Processes from key areas like quality control and risk management, on the other hand, are being moved to a secondary level, which is concerning. These findings might be essential in supporting researchers and practitioners in improving project management performance, given the poor track record of IS initiatives in terms of success.
